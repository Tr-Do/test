ACM TAMUSA

We aim to connect students across disciplines by fostering collaboration, innovation, and personal growth through a shared interest in solving real-world problems with applied computing skills.


Who we are:

  • A student-led organization dedicated to technology, creativity, and innovation.
  • A welcoming space for all majors and skill levels, from tech enthusiasts to aspiring professionals.
  • A network of peers supporting one another in academic, career, and personal development.
  • A hub for hands-on experiences in coding, cybersecurity, data science, and more.
  • A launchpad for growth through events, mentorship, projects, and competitions.

Whether you're exploring tech for the first time or ready to lead your next big project, ACM TAMUSA is here to help you connect, learn, and thrive.

ACM-W

Our mission is to empower and uplift students, with a particular focus on supporting women in technology, by fostering an inlcusive, dynamic, and collaborative environment.


We strive to:

  • Empower women and underrepresented students to thrive in tech though mentorship, leadership roles, and skill-building opportunities.
  • Enhance academic and professional development via workshops, career panels, speaker vents, and resume reviews.
  • Foster social connections and a sense of belonging through mixers, game nights, and team-building activities.
  • Building a vibrant, supportive network of peers, alumni, and industry professionals.
  • Promote confidence and curiosity, encouraging members to explore new areas of computing and push boundaries.

Whether you're a beginner or an experienced coder join us in shaping a tech community that's not only intelligent and capable—but inclusive, inspiring, and fun.

What we offer

Workshops

Hands-on learning sessions designed to help students build technical skills and explore new technologies in a beginner-friendly environment.

Topics may include:

  • Web Development
  • Machine Learning
  • Cloud computing basics

Tech Nights

Community-driven events focused on tech talks, coding challenges, and collaborative projects. Meet peers and learn new things outside the classroom.

Expect:

  • Tech talks
  • Coding competitions
  • Open source projects

Hackathons

Intensive coding marathons where students team up to build projects in a short time. Open to all skill levels!

Highlights:

  • Team-based problem-solving
  • Networking with industry mentors
  • Prizes, swags, and fun challenges

Volunteer

Opportunities to give back using your tech skills. Support events, mentor peers, or local outreach programs.

Get involved in:

  • Mentorship programs
  • Youth camps
  • Tech for good initiatives
